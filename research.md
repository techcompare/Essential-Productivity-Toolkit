This section covers tools and systems for effective reading, research, and information management.

Research productivity involves organizing sources, taking smart notes, and building a personal knowledge management system for long-term learning.

## Resources

- [Zotero](https://www.zotero.org) - Free, open-source reference manager for collecting and organizing research sources.
- [Mendeley](https://www.mendeley.com) - Reference manager and academic social network for researchers.
- [EndNote](https://endnote.com) - Reference management software for academic research and bibliography creation.
- [Paperpile](https://paperpile.com) - Modern reference manager integrated with Google Docs and Google Scholar.
- [ReadCube Papers](https://www.papersapp.com) - Reference management with PDF annotation and citation tools.
- [JabRef](https://www.jabref.org) - Open-source bibliography reference manager for LaTeX users.
- [Notion](https://www.notion.so) - All-in-one workspace for organizing research notes and sources.
- [Obsidian](https://obsidian.md) - Knowledge base for connecting research notes with bidirectional linking.
- [Roam Research](https://roamresearch.com) - Note-taking tool designed for research and networked thought.
- [Logseq](https://logseq.com) - Open-source knowledge management and collaboration platform.
- [RemNote](https://www.remnote.com) - Note-taking tool with spaced repetition for memorizing research.
- [Evernote](https://evernote.com) - Note-taking app with powerful search and web clipping for research.
- [OneNote](https://www.onenote.com) - Digital notebook from Microsoft for organizing research and notes.
- [Bear](https://bear.app) - Beautiful writing app for notes and research on Apple devices.
- [Craft](https://www.craft.do) - Document editor for notes with backlinking and beautiful formatting.
- [Google Scholar](https://scholar.google.com) - Search engine for scholarly literature across disciplines.
- [Semantic Scholar](https://www.semanticscholar.org) - AI-powered research tool for scientific literature.
- [ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net) - Social networking site for scientists and researchers.
- [Academia.edu](https://www.academia.edu) - Platform for sharing and discovering academic research papers.
- [JSTOR](https://www.jstor.org) - Digital library of academic journals, books, and primary sources.
- [PubMed](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov) - Free search engine for biomedical and life sciences literature.
- [arXiv](https://arxiv.org) - Open-access archive for scientific papers in various fields.
- [SSRN](https://www.ssrn.com) - Repository for early-stage research in social sciences and humanities.
- [Connected Papers](https://www.connectedpapers.com) - Visual tool for exploring academic papers and finding related work.
- [Consensus](https://consensus.app) - AI-powered search engine that finds answers in research papers.
- [Elicit](https://elicit.org) - AI research assistant that finds and summarizes papers.
- [Scite](https://scite.ai) - Smart citations that show how publications have been cited.
- [Pocket](https://getpocket.com) - Save articles and research papers to read later offline.
- [Instapaper](https://www.instapaper.com) - Save web pages for reading later with clean, readable formatting.
- [Raindrop.io](https://raindrop.io) - Bookmark manager for organizing research sources and references.
- [Pinboard](https://pinboard.in) - Fast, no-nonsense bookmarking site for archiving web pages.
- [Hypothesis](https://web.hypothes.is) - Web annotation tool for highlighting and discussing online texts.
- [Diigo](https://www.diigo.com) - Social bookmarking with highlighting, sticky notes, and annotations.
- [Liner](https://getliner.com) - Web highlighter for marking important text on any webpage.
- [Readwise](https://readwise.io) - Resurface highlights from books, articles, and documents with spaced repetition.
- [Matter](https://hq.getmatter.app) - Reading app that helps you read better and remember more.
- [Reader by Readwise](https://readwise.io/read) - All-in-one reading app for articles, newsletters, and PDFs.
- [Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com) - Track reading progress and discover books based on research interests.
- [LibraryThing](https://www.librarything.com) - Catalog your book collection and connect with other readers.
- [Calibre](https://calibre-ebook.com) - Open-source ebook management and reading tool.
- [Kindle](https://www.amazon.com/kindle) - E-reader and app for reading books with highlighting and notes.
- [Apple Books](https://www.apple.com/apple-books) - E-reader app for Apple devices with annotation features.
- [Google Books](https://books.google.com) - Search and preview books from around the world.
- [Open Library](https://openlibrary.org) - Digital library with millions of free books and lending.
- [Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org) - Free ebooks of public domain literature and classic texts.
- [CORE](https://core.ac.uk) - Aggregator of open access research papers from repositories worldwide.
- [BASE](https://www.base-search.net) - Search engine for academic web resources and open access papers.
- [WorldCat](https://www.worldcat.org) - Global catalog of library collections for finding books and resources.
- [How to Read a Book](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/567610.How_to_Read_a_Book) - Classic guide on analytical and critical reading techniques.
- [How to Take Smart Notes](https://takesmartnotes.com) - Guide to the Zettelkasten method for note-taking and research.
- [The Craft of Research](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/C/bo23521678.html) - Comprehensive guide to research methodology and writing.
